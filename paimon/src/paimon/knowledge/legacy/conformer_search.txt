Find stable conformers of a given molecule is an extremely tricky problem.
First, using open babel is better than rdkit in aspect of speed.
You should
1) create very large amount of conformers by searching algorithm,
2) remove duplicates (in terms of rmsd),
3) and get physically meaningful final conformers by minimization.
Also, aggressive search to escape global or local minima is indispensive.
For this, you should iteratively search new conformers from previously generated conformers.
Also you should check the final conformers have truely different geometries.
Here, you should consider
  - permutation of equivalent atoms
  - ignoring hydrogen (they are too soft)
  - large rmsd among conformers
  - existence of dihedral angle that largely differs among conformers
If you fail, you should go back to the initial step, reflecting any missing logic in your code.
