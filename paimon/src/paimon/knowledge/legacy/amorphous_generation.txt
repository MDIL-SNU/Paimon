The generation of amorphous structure from the given crystal structure is based on the melt-quench process. First the crystal structure is heated up at temperature much higher than melting point, to erase crystalline information during the short MD time. This step is called pre-melting. Next, the pre-melt state is instantly cooled down to temperatures slightly higher than the melting point to obtain the equilibrated liquid state. Afterwards, the liquid is gradually quenched to the room temperature. The final structure is obtained by fully relaxing the quenched state at 0 K.
If user do not request specific dimension of the amorphous structure, then default target structure is around 100 atom with cubic box (equal and orthogonal edges). The default density is 15% smaller than that of crystal. The initial structure for pre-melting is obtained by multiplying the unit cell in each lattice direction and then scaling the supercell uniformly. The multiplication and scaling values are determined to satisfy the following conditions as much as possible: 1) number of atoms 2) density 3) dimension if any of these conditions 1)~3) are imposed by user or system default settings. 4) The resulting lattice lengths are similar to each other. After pre-melting, we add MD steps to gradually morph the lattice vectors into the final target lattice vectors. Then, the melting step continues.
