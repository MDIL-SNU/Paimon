## Preparation: Build Argyrodite Structures and Select Targets

### Define compositions and enforce charge neutrality
1. Set the general formula to Li24+xM4S20X4 with M ∈ {Si, Ge, Sn, P, As, Sb} and X ∈ {Cl, Br, I}.
2. Enforce charge neutrality by assuming anion charges S2− and X−; adjust the Li count (x) accordingly to satisfy overall neutrality.
   - Negative constraint: Do not assume other anion charge states when computing x.

### Place atoms on Wyckoff sites
1. Place 24 Li atoms at the 24g Wyckoff positions.
2. If the composition requires Li beyond 24, randomly distribute the excess Li over the 48h and 16e sites.
   - Conditional branch: Only place excess Li if Li count > 24; otherwise skip.
3. Place the 4 M cations at the 4b sites.
4. Place S at the 16e sites to form MS4 tetrahedra.

### Apply anion-site disorder (S/X disorder)
1. Target an anion disorder level from {0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% X@4c}.
2. For the chosen X@4c fraction, randomly assign X to the 4c sites to achieve the specified percentage.
3. Fill remaining anion sites with S.
   - Negative constraint: Do not apply the heating (“300→1200 K”) trajectories described below to compute diffusivity; they are only for PES benchmarking.
   - Implicit sequencing: Assign disorder before any relaxation or MD.

### Initial cell dimensions and symmetry
1. Use the primitive argyrodite unit cell (≈ 10 Å on a side) to minimize periodic self-interaction in later MD.
2. Retain the cubic cell shape for argyrodite systems throughout structural relaxation and subsequent steps.
   - Negative constraint: Do not relax the cell shape away from cubic for argyrodites.

## DFT Static Relaxation (0 K)

### Convergence determination (prior to production runs)
1. Choose plane-wave cutoff energy and k-point mesh empirically to satisfy all:
   - Total energy convergence within 10 meV/atom.
   - Force convergence within 0.02 eV/Å.
   - Stress convergence within 10 kbar.
2. Use these converged settings for all production relaxations.
   - Implicit sequencing: Determine convergence before launching production relaxations.

### Production relaxation
1. Use PAW pseudopotentials and the PBE exchange–correlation functional.
2. Relax atomic positions until the maximum force is below 0.04 eV/Å.
3. Keep the cell shape cubic (fixed-shape relaxation).
4. Save the relaxed structure for MD.
   - Implicit sequencing: Use the relaxed structure as the starting point for both DFT-MD and MLIP-MD.

## AIMD for Li Diffusivity (DFT-MD at Fixed Temperatures)

### Simulation setup
1. Use the relaxed 0 K structure as input.
2. Use Γ-point electronic sampling.
3. Run NVT ensemble with a Nosé–Hoover thermostat.
4. Set the time step to 2 fs.
   - Conditional branch: If hydrogen is present (not the case for argyrodites), use 1 fs; otherwise keep 2 fs.
5. Choose fixed temperatures: 800 K, 1000 K, and 1200 K.

### Trajectory generation
1. For each temperature, run 5 independent trajectories of 100 ps each.
2. Assign different random initial velocities for each replicate at a given temperature.
   - Negative constraint: Do not reuse identical velocity seeds across replicates.
3. Record atomic positions at every MD step (2 fs) or at a cadence sufficient for MSD computation (retain full-resolution data if storage permits).
4. Repeat for each target composition.

## MLIP-based MD for Li Diffusivity (Optional Alternative to AIMD)

### Model selection
1. Use either:
   - The pretrained SevenNet-0 universal MLIP (release dated 2024-07-11), or
   - A fine-tuned SevenNet-0 variant (reEWC) trained on Li6PS5Cl (LPSC) as described below (optional, if reproducing MLIP-based results).

### Optional: Fine-tune SevenNet-0 with reEWC (LPSC dataset)
1. Build the LPSC fine-tuning dataset:
   - Composition/configuration: Li6PS5Cl with 50% Cl@4c (two Cl at the 4a and 4c sites; other anion positions filled by S).
   - Generate AIMD trajectories at 600 K and 1000 K (NVT, Nosé–Hoover, Γ-point, 2 fs, 100 ps per temperature).
   - Sample snapshots every 200 fs (500 frames per temperature, 1000 total).
   - Split into train/validation with a 9:1 random split.
2. Training configuration:
   - Optimizer: Adam.
   - Total epochs: 400.
   - Learning-rate schedule: Cosine annealing with linear warm-up.
     - Warm-up: increase from 0 to 1e−4 over the first 50 epochs.
     - Decay: from 1e−4 to 0 over the next 150 epochs; repeat 2 cycles to reach 400 epochs.
   - Loss: Huber loss with δ = 0.01.
   - Total loss includes:
     - Energy term on per-atom normalized energy,
     - Force term with weight λF = 1,
     - Stress term with weight λS = 0.01.
3. EWC/reEWC regularization and Replay:
   - EWC λ = 1e6; reEWC λ = 1e5.
   - Fisher Information Matrix (diagonal approximation): compute on 1,154,095 pretrained configurations with Huber loss < 0.000726.
   - Replay set: 10% random sample of the MPtrj dataset; use mini-batch alternation (fine-tuning batch followed by Replay batch of equal size).
   - Negative constraint: Do not use Replay-only (without EWC) if MD stability across chemistries is critical; reEWC is used to constrain parameter drift.

### MLIP-MD simulation setup (LAMMPS)
1. Use the relaxed 0 K DFT structure of the target argyrodite as the initial geometry.
2. Use NVT ensemble with a Nosé–Hoover thermostat.
3. Set the time step to 2 fs.
4. Choose fixed temperatures: 800 K, 1000 K, and 1200 K.
5. For each temperature, run 5 independent trajectories of 100 ps each with different random initial velocities.
6. Use a simulation cell with linear dimensions ≈ 10 Å to minimize periodic self-interaction.
   - Negative constraint: Avoid substantially smaller cells that increase periodic self-interaction artifacts.

## Analysis: Li Self-Diffusivity Extraction from MD

### Compute time-averaged MSD (multi–time-origin)
1. For each trajectory and temperature, compute the mean-squared displacement (MSD) for Li ions using the time-averaged method (multiple time origins).
2. Use the definition:
   - MSD(τ) = ⟨ (1/N) Σi |ri(t + τ) − ri(t)|² ⟩t,
   where N is the number of Li ions, ri(t) is the position of the ith Li at time t, and ⟨·⟩t denotes averaging over time origins.

### Trim non-diffusive and low-statistics regions
1. Discard the initial “ballistic” region where MSD < 4.5 Å² (apply the same cutoff at all temperatures).
2. Discard data beyond 70% of the total simulation time (retain only the first 70% after ballistic trimming).

### Fit the Einstein relation and compute D
1. Fit the trimmed MSD(t) data to the Einstein relation:
   - D = (1 / (6 t)) × MSD(t).
2. Extract D from the linear fit slope for each trajectory.

### Aggregate across replicates and report
1. For each composition and temperature, compute the mean of D over the 5 independent runs.
2. Compute the standard deviation across the 5 runs.
3. Report the mean and standard deviation for each composition at 800 K, 1000 K, and 1200 K.

## Optional Stability Diagnostic: Quasi-melting Consistency Check

### Detect quasi-melting events
1. For each composition at 1000 K and 1200 K, run 5 independent MD trajectories (as above).
2. Compute the MSD rate (slope of MSD vs time) for all non-Li ions.
3. Flag a trajectory as quasi-melted if all non-Li ions have MSD rate > 0.36 Å²/ps.
   - Negative constraint: Do not classify as quasi-melting if only a subset of non-Li ions exceed the threshold.
4. Compute the quasi-melting ratio as the fraction of the 5 runs that satisfy the criterion.

### Interpret results
1. Use DFT-MD as the reference behavior when available.
2. Compare MLIP-MD quasi-melting ratios to DFT-MD to identify PES softening (overprediction) or stiffening (underprediction) pathologies.

## PES Benchmark Trajectories (Do Not Use for Diffusivity)

### Generate PES snapshots for parity/softening analysis (optional context)
1. Optimize the structure at 0 K as above.
2. Run a single NVT AIMD trajectory:
   - Nosé–Hoover thermostat, Γ-point sampling, 2 fs time step, total duration 100 ps.
   - Gradually heat from 300 K to 1200 K over the 100 ps.
3. Sample structures every 200 fs (500 snapshots per composition).
4. Negative constraint: Do not use these heating snapshots to compute diffusivity; use only fixed-temperature trajectories for D extraction.
