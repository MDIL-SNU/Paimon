Pro tips for atomistic simulations

1. System Setup and Preparation

    Does the system need structural relaxation before property calculations?

    Is the simulation cell large enough to avoid artificial interactions?
    (Especially critical for surfaces, defects, and charged systems.)

    When simulating slabs or molecules, is there sufficient vacuum spacing if PBC is forced (like VASP)? or PBC itself is off? (possible for MLIP)
    (Typically ≥15 Å for slabs or isolated molecules.)

    Are the boundary conditions appropriate for the system being modeled?
    (3D periodic for bulk, 2D periodic + vacuum for surfaces, non-periodic for clusters or a molecule.)

    Any atom pairs that too close each other?
    (Atoms could fly away right after the simulation begins.)

    Is the additional dispersion correction required?
    (It might be crucial for systems including organic system or surface.)

2. Numerical Convergence and Accuracy

    Have I tested convergence with respect to key parameters?
    (E.g., supercell size, vacuum spacing, k-point sampling, cutoff energy, time step, etc.)

    Are the calculated properties insensitive to simulation settings?
    (Convergence of energy does not guarantee convergence of forces or derived quantities.)

    Do I need to sample multiple configurations or initial conditions?
    (Especially important in finite-temperature MD or disordered systems.)

3. Simulation Planning and Execution

    What is the physical question I’m trying to answer — and is the method suitable for it?
    (E.g., static calculations for structural stability vs. MD for transport properties.)

    Do I need finite-temperature sampling, or is a single-point calculation enough?
    (Temperature effects are crucial for entropy-dominated phenomena.)

    Is the simulation long enough (or ensemble diverse enough) to reach meaningful averages?
    (Thermodynamic properties require statistical convergence.)

4. Units
    
    Are all input and output quantities expressed in consistent and physically meaningful units?
    (Defaults to, length [Å or Bohr], energy [eV or Ha], time [fs], force [eV/Å], stress [GPa or eV/Å³], and temperature [K].)

    Are any conversions needed between codes or data formats?
    (Some packages use atomic units internally — be cautious when transferring data between codes.)

    Is the choice of units appropriate for interpreting the results physically?
    (E.g., express diffusion in Å²/ps for MD output, but convert to cm²/s for experimental comparison.)

    When outputting a file associated with physical values, is the unit explicitly specified?
