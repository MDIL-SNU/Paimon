**Universal Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (uMLIPs)**

uMLIPs are pre-trained, machine-learning interatomic potentials that predict energy, forces, and stress tensors directly from atomic positions and chemical species.
They represents high accuracy potential energy surfaces across a wide range of systems without the need for fine-tuning or system-specific training data.
They cover broad chemical space without system-specific training data, functioning as drop-in replacements for classical force fields or expensive DFT calculations. Examples include SevenNet and MACE.

If the user does not explicitly specify a force field or interatomic potential, you must always use a uMLIP.
You may use the retrieve_umlip_knowledge tool to obtain model-specific information (e.g., supported elements, training domain, accuracy characteristics). Only uMLIPs supported by this tool are allowed.
By default, you must first examine the SevenNet family and select the most appropriate recommended model from that family unless there is a clear reason (grounded in retrieved knowledge) to use another supported uMLIP.
You must explicitly state which uMLIP (including model name/version, if applicable) is selected and briefly justify the choice based on its documented scope or capabilities.

## What uMLIPs CAN Do

- Total energy, atomic forces, and stress tensor
- D3 dispersion for van der Waals interactions can be explicitly included where required
- Accurately describes both short- and long-range interactions
- (Mostly) No manual charge assignment is required — just place the atoms, and the force field will automatically capture the correct behavior based on the local environment

## What uMLIPs CANNOT Do 
Regard below things are not supported by uMLIPs, unless the official documentation of a specific model explicitly states otherwise.

### Electronic Structure Properties
- uMLIPs predict only quantities derivable from the potential energy surface. They cannot compute any property that requires explicit electronic structure.

### Charged Systems
- Most uMLIPs are trained exclusively on charge-neutral DFT calculations.
- Exceptions include MACE-OMOL which supports charge embedding.

### Spin States and Magnetic Properties
- Most uMLIPs cannot set spin states explicitly and are restricted to the spin configurations present in their training data.
- Exceptions include MACE-OMOL which supports spin embedding, and SevenNet-Omni which includes inference task of both `omol25_low` and `omol25_high`, representing low- and hihg-spin states.

### Coarse-Grained Simulations
- uMLIPs operate at the atomistic level only. Every atom must be explicitly represented.

### Excited States
- uMLIPs are trained on ground-state DFT data and cannot model excited electronic states.


## Inherited DFT Functional Limitations
- uMLIPs learn potential energy surfaces from DFT reference data. Any systematic error or bias present in the reference functional is inherited by the trained model. This is not a training fault but a fundamental limitation of the level of theory.
- PBE-trained uMLIPs inherit PBE's systematic errors (e.g., poor description of van der Waals interactions without dispersion correction, over-estimation of lattice constants in solids)
- r2SCAN-trained uMLIPs inherit r2SCAN's characteristics (generally better geometries and energetics for many materials)
- When experimental validation is critical, choose a model trained on the functional most appropriate for your target property


## Computational Guidance

- GPU execution is strongly recommended. CPU execution is prohibitively slow for systems with more than ~100 atoms.
- Model size tradeoff: Where variants exist, smaller models are faster but less accurate; larger models are slower but more accurate. Choose based on the accuracy requirements of the task.
