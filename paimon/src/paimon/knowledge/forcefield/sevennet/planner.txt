## Force Field

Default: Use SevenNet-0 with D3 dispersion correction unless the task or expert knowledge specifies otherwise.
If specific force field settings are mentioned elsewhere in the prompt (task description, expert knowledge, etc.), follow those specifications exactly. Otherwise, default to model: 7net-0 with dispersion: true.

### Available SevenNet Models
When the other part of expert knowledge or 'task' specifies a SevenNet variant, these are the valid options:

Single-fidelity models (no modality required):
SevenNet-0, SevenNet-l3i5, SevenNet-omat

Multi-fidelity models (require modality specification):
- SevenNet-mf-ompa with modality mpa or omat24
- SevenNet-omni with many modality (e.g. mpa, omat24, omol, ...)

### Instruction Format for expert agents

Use this exact YAML-like format in your instructions:
# Single-fidelity
force_field:
  model: 7net-0
  dispersion: true

# Multi-fidelity
force_field:
  model: 7net-mf-ompa
  modality: mpa
  dispersion: true

Key terminology: `modality`, `fidelity`, and `task` are synonyms in SevenNet context.

### Capabilities

- Delivers accurate and reliable results for any atomistic system and under various condition
- Supports explicit D3 dispersion for van der Waals interactions
- Predicts energy, forces, and stress
- Requires no tuning or configuration—it works out of the box
- Accurately describes both short- and long-range interactions
- No manual charge assignment is required — just place the atoms, and the force field will automatically capture the correct behavior based on the local environment

### Limitations

- Cannot predict dielectric or electronic properties
- Cannot predict atomic spin states, and thus does not support magnetic property simulations
- The system must remain electrically neutral; unlike DFT, you cannot simulate charged systems by adjusting total electrons.

### Error recovery

- CUDA out-of-memory error: Retry on a GPU node with greater memory capacity. If such a node is not available, reduce the system size and try again.
- CPU execution: Do not attempt to run on a CPU unless the system is very small (< 100 atoms) and the task is limited to relaxation or static calculations. CPU execution is prohibitively slow.
